# Work Permit Regulations

## Section 1: Definitions

**Work permit** means a written authorization to work in Canada issued to a foreign national by an officer.

**Employer** means a person or organization that employs or engages a foreign national to perform work.

## Section 2: Eligibility Criteria

### 2.1 General Requirements

A foreign national is eligible for a work permit if they:

(a) have a valid job offer from a Canadian employer;

(b) meet the requirements of the job offer;

(c) are not inadmissible on grounds of criminality or security;

(d) have sufficient means to support themselves and their family members in Canada.

### 2.2 Labour Market Impact Assessment

An employer must obtain a positive Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) before hiring a foreign worker, except where an exemption applies under paragraph R204(a) or (c).

## Section 3: Application Process

### 3.1 Employer Requirements

Before a foreign worker can apply for a work permit, the employer must:

(a) demonstrate that the job offer meets prevailing wage rates;

(b) show that the employment will not adversely affect Canadian workers;

(c) provide a written job offer on company letterhead.

### 3.2 Officer Assessment

The officer must be satisfied that:

(a) the work is genuine and the employer can fulfill the job offer;

(b) the foreign national will depart Canada at the end of the authorized period;

(c) the foreign national will comply with all conditions of the permit.

## Section 4: Conditions

### 4.1 Standard Conditions

Every work permit is subject to the following conditions:

(a) the holder must work only for the employer specified in the permit;

(b) the holder must not work beyond the period of employment authorized;

(c) the holder must leave Canada when the permit expires.

### 4.2 Duration

A work permit may be issued for a period not exceeding:

(a) the period of employment specified in the LMIA;

(b) in the case of an LMIA-exempt work permit, the period authorized by the regulations.

## Section 5: Extensions

An application for extension of a work permit must be made before the permit expires. The officer may grant an extension if:

(a) the employer continues to meet the requirements;

(b) the foreign national continues to meet the requirements;

(c) the work continues to be consistent with the original purpose.

## Section 6: Compliance

### 6.1 Employer Obligations

An employer who hires a foreign worker must:

(a) comply with all terms and conditions of the employment;

(b) maintain records of the foreign worker's employment;

(c) provide the foreign worker with wages and working conditions equal to those provided to Canadian workers.

### 6.2 Consequences of Non-Compliance

Where an employer fails to comply with the conditions:

(a) the employer may be subject to administrative penalties;

(b) the employer's LMIA application may be refused;

(c) the foreign worker's permit may be revoked.
